Palma de Mallorca

Palma: the elegant capital city of Mallorca, the largest of the Balearic Islands, a sparkling archipelago in the western Mediterranean Sea. Rich in history, Palma is a cosmopolitan centre of culture, where you can lose yourself in the labyrinthine streets and vibrant island-city ambience.

Top 3 Activities

VISIT PALMA CATHEDRAL

Palma de Mallorca’s star attraction is La Seu, the magnificent Gothic cathedral that stands proudly on the edge of the Old Town overlooking the Mediterranean Sea. Constructed from sandstone between 1230 and 1600, La Seu demands a visit: exquisite carved exteriors, spectacular stained glass windows and recent additions by world-renowned Spanish artists, Antoni Gaudí and Miquel Barceló.

ENJOY A HOT CHOCOLATE AT CAN JOAN DE S’AIGO

From the cathedral, walk the winding cobblestone streets of the Old Town to Ca’n Joan de s’Aigo, a cosy chocolatier founded in 1700 and steeped in Mallorcan history. It’s a family-owned business that oozes old-world charm, with uniformed waiters serving traditional handmade pastries, ice cream, and steaming hot chocolate.

TAPAS AT MARCAT D’OLIVAR

Don’t miss a visit to the best market in central Palma: Mercat d’Olivar. Popular with locals and restaurant owners, who source the ripest seasonal fruit and vegetables here, the market is a true sensory experience and an authentic taste of Mallorca.

Top 3 Restaurents

Kasui (Japanese)

An intimate Japanese grill and cocktail bar in downtown Palma, Kasui has black and red interiors indicative of the country that inspires its cuisine. The glowing neon wall sign aptly describes the owners' philosophy: Eat it, drink it, share it; and the menu is divided into different sections: izakaya—a traditional Japanese drinking-and-dining den concept—alongside charcoal- grilled dishes known as robatayaki, kushiyaki (grilled skewers) and assorted street food.

Tast Club

Down a narrow side street off the central boulevard of Paseo del Borne, one of Palma’s most esteemed restaurants can be found underground in a warm, intimate space that recalls a private member’s club. Elegant cocktails are mixed at the marble bar lit by a grand chandelier hung from the vaulted ceiling. Tast offers a refined menu, a low-lit ambience and glamorous clientele making this restaurant one of Palma’s not-so-best-kept secrets.

DINS by Santi Taura

For Majlorcan cuisine with a contemporary twist, Dins offers a gastronomic experience taking fresh, local ingredients that chef, Santi Tauri, interprets into unique dishes. Located in the Calatrava neighbourhood close to the cathedral, Dins has a stellar reputation verified by two Repsol Suns and a Michelin star.

TIPS

The beach promenade of Palma de Mallorca, locally known as Paseo Marítimo, is such a peaceful passageway in winter. You can take a stroll under the palm trees and admire the fancy boats at the harbour without needing to mind any beggars, hawkers, pickpockets or even other tourists. The promenade stretches for more than five kilometres allowing you to spend a good hour or two just walking, biking, rollerblading or skateboarding along the beach - and perhaps enjoying somelovely tapas and cañas on the way
